About these demos

You're looking at a Krevio demo.

Every "business" on a Krevio demo page — the plumber, the landscaper, the HVAC company, the REALTOR® — is fictional. The dashboards, receipts, reviews, and booking flows you see are mocked. The code, the AI chatbot, and the infrastructure are real — the same stack we ship to paying customers.

Why it looks so real

Because the product is real. Krevio builds production websites + AI chatbots + lead capture for local service businesses. The demos exist so a business owner can click through a working example of the thing they'd be buying before they buy it.

What's fictional vs what's not

  • Fictional: business names, phone numbers (all 555- format by convention), street addresses, reviewer names, testimonial text, specific license numbers, dashboard pipeline data, booking availability, and any "transactions" you see.
  • Real: the chatbot (Gemini 2.5 Flash), the voice responses (Google Cloud TTS), the AI lead-qualification logic, the hosting (Vercel), the design tokens, the code itself. Everything under the hood.

The chatbot knows it's a demo

If you ask the chatbot "are you real?" or try to book an appointment, it'll answer truthfully: it's a demo assistant, not a real business. It won't invent license numbers, won't commit to bookings, and won't pretend to be a human. That's intentional — public demos need to be honest about what they are.
